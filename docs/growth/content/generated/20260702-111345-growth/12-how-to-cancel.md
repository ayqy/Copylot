Cancelling a subscription, order, or service can be tricky. This guide covers the exact steps, the most common mistakes people make, and what to do when the cancellation doesn't go through. No fluff, just actionable solutions.

## What Does 'How to Cancel' Really Mean?

When you search "how to cancel," you usually mean: *How do I end a subscription, order, or ongoing service without getting charged again or losing access prematurely?* The answer depends on the type of service and its specific cancellation policy. This guide works for most digital subscriptions, SaaS tools, streaming services, and recurring physical goods.

## Step-by-Step Cancellation Guide

Follow these steps in order. Skipping ahead may cause extra charges or reactivation difficulties.

### Step 1: Find the Cancellation Policy
- Look for "Cancel Subscription," "Manage Plan," or "Billing" in your account settings.
- Read the cancellation terms: What is the notice period? Is there a refund? Is cancellation immediate or at the end of the billing cycle?
- If you can't find it, search the help center for the service name + "cancel policy."

### Step 2: Locate the Cancellation Button or Contact Method
- In most services, cancellation is under **Account > Subscription > Cancel**.
- Some services require an email or phone call. For example, gym memberships or annual contracts often need written notice.
- If the option is hidden, try switching to a desktop browser — many apps hide cancellation in the web version.

### Step 3: Confirm and Capture Evidence
- After initiating cancellation, you should see a confirmation message or email. **Save this.**
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation screen and keep the email until the next billing date passes.
- If the service asks why you're leaving, it's okay to answer, but it's not mandatory. A simple "no longer needed" works.

### Step 4: Verify Cancellation
- Check your account status (still shows "Active"? That's common until the end of the billing period).
- Remove payment method if possible — but be careful: some services may reactivate your subscription if you re-login or update billing info.
- Mark your calendar for the next expected charge date. If charged again, you have proof to dispute.

## Common Mistakes That Can Ruin Your Cancellation

- **Mistake #1: Only deleting the app or unsubscribing from emails.** This does not cancel the subscription. You must cancel through the official billing settings.
- **Mistake #2: Assuming a free trial auto-cancels.** Most trials auto-renew. Set a reminder to cancel at least 48 hours before the trial ends.
- **Mistake #3: Not reading the fine print about notice periods.** Some services require 30 days' notice. If you cancel on the last day of the billing period, you may still be charged for the next month.
- **Mistake #4: Closing the account instead of canceling the subscription.** On some platforms, closing the account also cancels, but on others, you need to cancel first, then close. Check the help article.

## What If Cancellation Fails?

If you followed the steps but still get charged or the subscription remains active:

1. **Contact customer support** — Use live chat or email. Be polite and provide your confirmation screenshot.
2. **Dispute the charge** — If you have proof of cancellation and they still charge, dispute with your bank or payment provider (e.g., PayPal or credit card chargeback).
3. **Try an alternative method** — Some services have different cancellation paths for web vs. mobile. If one fails, try the other.
4. **Use a temporary virtual card or pause the card** — As a last resort, if the service refuses to cancel, you may need to block future payments by canceling the card or using a service like Privacy.com.

## When None of This Works

If the service is unresponsive or continues billing, you may need to escalate to consumer protection agencies (like the FTC in the US) or your local equivalent. In most cases, a clear paper trail of your cancellation request will get you a refund.

## FAQ

### How to cancel 适合谁？

This guide is for anyone trying to cancel a recurring subscription or service: SaaS tools, streaming platforms, gym memberships, or any auto-renewing plan. It's not for one-time purchases or free trials that have already expired.

### How to cancel 最容易踩的坑是什么？

The biggest mistake is thinking cancelling means deleting the app or unsubscribing from emails. That doesn't cancel the billing. Always go into your account settings, find the subscription section, and hit the cancel button. Another common trap is not reading the notice period — some services charge you for one more month even after you cancel.

### How to cancel 失败时的备用方案是什么？

If cancellation fails or you still get charged, first contact customer support with your cancellation confirmation (screenshot or email). If that doesn't work, dispute the charge with your bank or payment provider. As a last resort, you can block future payments by cancelling the credit card or using a temporary virtual card.
