## What Actually Matters When Setting Up as a First-Time Foreign Tourist

You land in a new country. You need internet, money access, and a way to navigate. Here's the real setup checklist.

### 1. Connectivity First
Most tourists rush to buy a local SIM at the airport. That can work, but it's often expensive and requires ID registration. Alternative: buy a travel eSIM before departure (e.g., Airalo, Holafly). You get data instantly, avoid language barriers, and keep your home number active for OTPs.

**Pitfall:** Some eSIMs don't support voice calls. Check if you need a local number for ride-hailing or restaurant reservations.

### 2. Money & Banking
Notify your home bank of travel dates to avoid card blocks. Carry at least one backup card (different network). For cash, use ATMs inside bank branches—standalone ATMs are more prone to skimming.

**Pitfall:** Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) at POS terminals is a hidden fee. Always choose to pay in local currency.

### 3. Documents & Digital Copies
Keep passport, visa, and travel insurance PDFs offline on your phone, and store copies in encrypted cloud storage. Paper copies in luggage are a good fallback.

**Pitfall:** Many services require original physical documents for SIM registration or hotel check-ins. Digital copies are not always accepted.

### 4. Transportation & Navigation
Google Maps works well in most countries, but transit data may be incomplete. Download offline maps for your destination region before arrival. Apps like Citymapper or Moovit are better for public transit in specific cities.

**Pitfall:** Roaming charges—if you rely on your home carrier's international plan, verify data caps and throttling policies.

### 5. Backup Plan When Things Fail
- If an eSIM doesn't activate: Find free public Wi-Fi (cafes, libraries, airports) and buy a physical SIM.
- If your card is declined: Use a prepaid travel card (e.g., Revolut, Wise) or withdraw cash at a bank counter with passport.
- If you lose your phone: Use a password manager on another device to recover credentials.

### Bottom Line
The best setup is redundant: have an eSIM + physical SIM, two cards, and offline copies of key docs. Always test your connectivity and card before leaving the airport.
